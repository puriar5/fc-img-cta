Our Goal

By 2030, every child has access to food education and nourishing food in school.

Why food in school

The case for food

Food is central to kids' physical health, emotional wellbeing, and sense of belonging. And schools are the opportune places for kids to connect to food through hands-on, experiential learning. We partner with schools and communities that have been most impacted by systemic racism and classism, serving urban, rural, and Indigenous communities where our collaboration can make a lasting difference.

The case for food

Our theory of action

THEORY 1

Prioritize food in school

FoodCorps helps school districts become national models for food education and school nutrition. Our AmeriCorps service members support districts that prioritize students learning about, growing, and eating nourishing food in school.

  • Building partnerships in communities where the majority of students count on school meals for their wellbeing

  • Providing people power, tools, and expertise to support schools and districts in creating food environments where students thrive, and documenting this impact through research and analysis

  • Inviting parents, local changemakers, and food activists to engage with and support our corps members’ and partner schools’ efforts

Theory 2

Invest in local leaders

Food educators, school nutrition leaders, and school food champions abound in every community. When we invest in finding, developing, and connecting these leaders, we ensure a direct impact on kids today and support changemakers and advocates who will impact many more kids in the future.

Our AmeriCorps program’s investment in next-generation leaders includes:

  • Convening service members, alums, and site partners through professional networks that nurture community and build leadership skills
  • Training and professional development for service members, leading to lifelong careers of purpose in food education, school nutrition, and policy
  • Recruiting local leaders who share backgrounds, identities, and lived experiences with the students who depend on school meals every day
theory 3

Change policy

We advocate for federal, state, and local policies that can make food education and nourishing food the norm across our nation’s 100,000 schools. We bring our on-the-ground experience and demonstrated community impact to the table, and we amplify the voices of alumni and other stakeholders who know the barriers that hold schools and districts back from achieving the full potential of food in school. Together with our partners, we pursue a bold vision for systemic change.

This work is guided by our policy vision for elevating the role of food in schools.
