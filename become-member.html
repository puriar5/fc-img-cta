As a FoodCorps service member, you can help kids learn what healthy food is, care where it comes from, and eat it every day. You’ll teach them to plant, grow, cook, and love it. FoodCorps recruits talented leaders for a year of full time paid public service building healthy school food environments in limited-resource communities.

@@title

What service members do

As a service member, you’ll implement FoodCorps’ evidence-based strategies to feed kids good food, teach them to know and love it, and help schools become healthier places for them to grow and learn. Most of your time will be spent teaching in the classroom and garden; there are also opportunities to work with school nutrition directors in supporting districtwide food systems change. Most service corps members do the following:

  • Lead hands-on lessons

    You’ll teach students to grow, cook, taste new foods, building their skills and enthusiasm, and changing their food preferences. You’ll collaborate with teachers on integrating food into the curriculum, and you may help organize field trips to local farms or visits from chefs.

  • Influence healthy school meals

    You’ll cultivate a cafeteria experience that steers students towards the healthiest options and gets them excited to try new foods. You’ll get kids to try samples during taste tests and brainstorm great ideas about good eating with them. You may even work with food service directors and farmers to get local foods onto the lunch line.

  • Build a schoolwide culture of health

    You’ll be an active, collaborative member of the school community, working to ensure that the whole school environment—from thallway to classroom to cafeteria—celebrates healthy food. You may plan family cooking nights and organize healthy fundraisers, too.

STEP 2

Who we’re looking for

You want to make a difference in the world. You understand that food is a driver of positive change. You see food as a fundamental substance of every kid’s education. And you want to work directly in schools, with students, to make food central to their classroom experience.

Eligibility

To be considered for a FoodCorps service member position, you must:

  • Be 18 years or older by the start of service
  • Be a U.S. citizen, U.S. national, or lawful permanent resident of the United States
  • Hold a high school diploma, GED or equivalent

The ideal candidate

  • Experience teaching or working with groups of kids
  • Experience gardening and/or farming, working with food, community organizing, studying food systems or agriculture, or working in public health
  • Commitment to public service
  • Connection to and/or knowledge of the community you want to serve
  • Understanding of FoodCorps’ mission and scope (school food, farm to school, race & class in the food system)
  • Potential to benefit from a year serving with FoodCorps

STEP 3

Where you’ll serve

FoodCorps currently serves schools and districts across the country in 12 states and the District of Columbia. You can choose up to three preferred states, and even specific service sites, in your application. We encourage you to serve in your local community, as we are looking to empower local leaders. Keep in mind that we do not provide housing.

Benefits of service

FoodCorps offers members a unique and transformative experience. You’ll make a measurable difference in the lives of your students and increasing equity in school systems while gaining professional skills that are transferable to many different career paths. And you’ll join a network of passionate, dedicated changemakers that will serve and support you no matter where your journey takes you in the future.

step 5

5 tips to make your application shine

1

Demonstrate knowledge about the community you want to serve in, or at least a community very similar to it.

2

Show your personality! We are looking for people with compelling personal stories, not cookie-cutter candidates.

3

Tell a story about a specific pivotal moment or experience that inspired you to pursue this path in your personal statement.

4

Upload a video or other supplemental file that expresses your passion for and connection to FoodCorps’ mission and what it means to you — be creative! We also encourage a good, personalized letter of recommendation.

Please note: You are welcome to include links as supplemental files. Just insert the link to your video, writing, or other content in a document you upload.

5

Connect with a FoodCorps staff member before applying in order to learn more about what service is like in your region of choice. Many of our program coordinators and managers are alums of the FoodCorps program. Because they have served with FoodCorps and are now supporting current service members on the ground, they have great insight into the service experience and how it varies from site to site. You can find contact information for the state coordinators, managers, and directors at the top of each state page.
